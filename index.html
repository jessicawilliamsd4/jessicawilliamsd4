About Jessica

Mom. Scientist.
Education Leader.

I'm Jessica Williams: mom of four and education leader. I've spent my entire career making schools work better for students who need it most.

A Georgia native, I earned my B.S. in Biology from Spelman College before going on to Princeton University for my Ph.D. in Molecular Biology. I know firsthand the power of exceptional education to transform a life, and I want every child in Henry County to have that same opportunity.

I've spent 15 years leading national education reform, helping hundreds of institutions improve student outcomes and building equity-centered systems that actually move the needle.

Henry County is growing, and the moment calls for serious leadership. A new STEM High School opens in 2027, a $600 million budget demands responsible stewardship, and thousands of children across this district deserve a board member with real expertise fighting for them every single day.

I'm not a career politician. I'm a mom, a Ph.D., and an education leader. All in for Henry County's kids.

My Priorities

What I'll fight for
on the board

Every decision I make on the board will start with one question: is this good for every child in Henry County? Here's where I'll focus.

01
🔬 Champion STEM Education

STEM is personal to me. My scientific training shapes how I think about education, and I'll use that perspective to champion our new STEM High School opening in 2027 and expand access to science and technology for every student in this district.

02
💰 Fiscal Responsibility

Managing a $596 million budget responsibly means planning for the long term. I'll bring the same financial discipline I've applied managing $100M+ in education funding to ensure Henry County Schools spends wisely and protects its future.

03
🛡️ School Safety Funding

The state funds only $58 per student for safety while Henry County spends $309, and state contributions are being cut. I'll be a relentless advocate at the state level to close this funding gap and ensure our children are protected in every school, every day.

04
🍎 Teacher Pay & Retention

Great schools require great teachers. Henry County has increased starting pay by 42% since 2018. We need to protect and build on that progress. I'll fight for competitive compensation, fight back against state mandates that eat into local flexibility, and ensure our teachers feel valued.
